Home Palette Design

Roles: Product Designer
Duration: December 2014 - Current

Description
I began this project as a way to illustrate the design process I tend to follow.

Home Design Palette is a web tool that facilitates online purchases of home furnishings by helping users color match potential buys with the palettes of their rooms.

Defining the Problem
When working at BednLinens, an online shop for home furnishings, part of my responsibiities included customer service. One of the common issues I encountered was that the customer would request a return on their purchase because upon the product's arrival, the customer would realize that the color of the product did not fit the color scheme of the room they wished to use it in. I realized these complaints could easily be alleviated if there were a simple way for the customer to match the color of the product to the color of their room.

With just an idea of what the problem I am trying to solve it, I need to speak with the customers to better understand what the solution sought. I put together a survey to ask different customer types about their shopping experience, needs, and habits. The results of the survey help to create user personas of the major subset of customer who will be using this tool. I usually try to describe 2-4 distinct personas. Then, for each of these characters, I imagine ten to twenty scenarios which they may find themselves in. The format is generally as such:

The user wants/needs _______.

By now, I will have a better understanding of the issue, and possibly a forming idea of possible solutions for it. I will want to take a moment to write up a document outlining the basics of my plan, answering questions about the objectives of the product. Knowing that the solution will be useful and usable provides the green light to begin designing the product.

Approach
With a focused problem to be solved, I may begin to outline the solution. I figure out what are the minimum features required to be able to deliver a working product. For HDP, a very top level concept of the final product requires a way for the user to upload or create a mock up of her room, select the furnishings she wants to customize, and alter the image such that the new decor may be applied and inspected. I will also consider additional features of lower priority based on survey suggestions that may be integrated if time permits.
