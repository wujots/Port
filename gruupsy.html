Gruupsy

Roles: UX Researcher, Front-End Dev
Duration: November 2014 - December 2014

Description
Gruupsy is a social web service that provides group discounts to activities in the San Diego area. It allows individuals to search for activities happening soon.
↳ The final layout of the landing page.

The Challenge
While there are services out there geared for business members to advertise to customers, Gruupsy wants students to be able to take the initiative to create their own events for which others are open to attend, as well gather groups to purchase tickets to events at a group discount. However, it would be difficult to convert users from existing platforms, and it was unclear whether they would be enthusiastic about having to put effort into contributing.

Approach
First, websites that offered similar services were examined for their technique and execution and the findings of the positives, what to avoid, and other extracted ideas were compiled into a competitive analysis report. Next, research had to be done to understand how users utilized existing services; surveys were conducted by watching how students interacted with sites such as Groupon, Massdrop, or the UCSD recreation site, and the problems were identified; these problems are what is to mainly be addressed in the first version of Gruupsy.

Content Creation
A sketched version of the potential site was created and evaluated among group members for possible areas of needing redesign. Based on the initial sketches and collected information about usage and competitors, wireframes were prototyped and created on paper and later, Balsamiq. Images chosen for the pages were high res to try to provide an immersive feeling to evoke what it could be like to attend some of the exciting events. Finally, the front end of the site was coded up based on the low fidelity wireframes.
